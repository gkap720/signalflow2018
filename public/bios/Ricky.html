Ricky Crews

Composer and performer, Ricky Crews (b.1989), grew up in rural Missouri. He received a BM degree in composition from Southeast Missouri State University, where he studied composition with Robert Fruehwald, saxophone with Michael Dean, and flute with Paul Thompson. The composer was then among the first to be admitted to the San Francisco Conservatory of Music’s newly-minted Technology and Applied Composition department. Now at Mills College, Crews focuses on repurposing the works of Berkley-based composer John Adams. In July 2017, Sarah Cahill and Crews performed 40 Years After China Gates, a reimagination of John Adams’s China Gates, written in 1977 for Cahill.
